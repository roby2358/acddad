# Using ACD

ACD is a tap-to-talk board. You build a line of text with big buttons, and the
tablet can speak it aloud. It's designed for a Samsung tablet held in landscape.

## The screen

- **Word display (top left):** the text you're building. A trailing `_` marks the
  end of the line, so you can see where the next letter lands — and whether a
  space is already there.
- **Speak button (top right, square):** speaks the whole line aloud.
  - **Speaker icon** = ready.
  - **Clock** = the voice is still starting up (wait a moment).
  - **Dot** = no voice available on this device (text still works, just no audio).
- **Switcher bar (tall bar on the right):** tap it to switch between the
  **letters** screen and the **words** screen. It has no label on purpose.

## Control row (top of both letter and word screens)

The same four buttons appear on both screens:

- **space** — adds a space. Does nothing if the line is empty or already ends
  with a space (so you can't make double spaces).
- **⌫** — deletes the last character.
- **clear word** — deletes the last word, back to the previous space.
- **clear** — erases the whole line.

## Letters screen

- **0–9** across the top, then **A–Z**.
- **YES** and **NO** (bottom right): these add the word to the line **and say it
  out loud right away**. (The full line is still only spoken by the Speak button,
  so YES/NO get said again when you press Speak.)

## Words screen

- **Common words** (e.g. help, pain, water, bathroom): tap to drop the whole word
  into the line.
- **Your most-used words** fill the bottom two rows. They're learned automatically
  from what you say and type, and they stay even after the app is closed. They
  start empty and fill in over time. Words already shown elsewhere (and YES/NO)
  won't be repeated here.
- **Remove a learned word:** tap the same one **5 times in a row** and it drops off
  the list. (It can come back if you use it a lot again.)

## Speaking

- Press **Speak** to hear the whole line.
- The voice is on-device, so it works without internet, and prefers a male voice.

## Tips

- Watch the `_` to see exactly where you are and whether you've added a space.
- To fix the last word, use **clear word** instead of tapping ⌫ many times.
